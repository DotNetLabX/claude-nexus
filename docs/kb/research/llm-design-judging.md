# llm-design-judging

## How do you make LLM judgment of software design quality reliable when no ground-truth oracle exists — comparative vs absolute scoring, panels vs single judge, known judge biases (position/verbosity/self-preference/authority), reject-by-default adversarial judging, and rubric-anchored criteria?

**Verdict:** Comparative/pairwise judging tracks human quality preference better than absolute scoring on subjective dimensions, but is markedly *more* gameable — pairwise verdicts flip ~35% of the time under adversarial "distractor" features vs ~9% for absolute scores. So an adversarial reject-by-default design gate should (1) score ABSOLUTELY against explicit, named rubric criteria with a hard *grounding* requirement — each cited pattern must map to the specific complexity-census rows it eliminates, converting a fuzzy holistic judgment into a checkable one — then (2) use pairwise ONLY to rank the survivors; (3) run a small heterogeneous panel of 3 disparate-family judges (never the generator model) aggregated robustly (geometric-median beats mean, which has unbounded bias under a single contaminated judge); and (4) treat authority/name-dropping bias as the primary threat, because LLM judges demonstrably credit unearned and even fabricated citations (robustness as low as 0.63). Panels help but hit fast diminishing returns — nine correlated judges are worth ~two independent votes.
**Evidence tier:** read-docs
**As-of:** 2026-07-11
**Validity scope:** LLM-as-judge field, frontier chat models used as judges, 2023–2026 literature (MT-Bench/Chatbot Arena era onward); bias *magnitudes* are model- and version-specific; nearly all primary evidence is on NLG/chat/code-correctness judging, NOT specifically software-design judging (see Caveat).
**Status:** current
**Reconfirm trigger:** A large-scale study specifically on LLM judging of software *design* quality (vs chat/NLG/code-correctness) landing; a frontier model shipping calibrated position/authority-bias resistance that closes the gaps below; or a panel-aggregation method superseding geometric-median.
**Corroboration:** high-stakes (a planned skill's stage-3 design rests on this). Multiple independent sources per load-bearing claim — pairwise-vs-absolute tension: Park/pairwise-pointwise study [2] + broad pairwise-alignment literature [8]; panel gains + diminishing returns: PoLL [3] + Nine-Judges [4] + RoPoLL [5] (three independent); biases: Zheng MT-Bench [1] + CALM/Justice-or-Prejudice [6] + Panickssery self-recognition [7] (three independent); reject-by-default precision/recall: jailbreak-judge calibration literature [8]; plus prior pool knowledge (CANDOR panel/grounding) [12].

## Verdict

There is no oracle for "good design," so the reliability question is entirely about *calibrating a subjective judge*. The literature gives a clear, and somewhat counterintuitive, decomposition for an adversarial gate:

**Comparative vs absolute is a robustness/alignment trade, not a strict ordering.** Pairwise/comparative judging is the more *human-aligned* protocol for subjective quality — relative judgments are easier and more stable than absolute scalars. But pairwise is also the more *exploitable* protocol: a generator can insert superficial distractor features that flip a pairwise verdict ~35% of the time, versus ~9% for absolute scoring, because absolute scoring anchors on the response's own quality rather than a head-to-head that a distractor can tip [2]. For an adversarial "kill unjustified proposals" gate, that inverts the usual advice: the KILL decision wants the robustness of absolute, rubric-anchored scoring; pairwise is best reserved for ranking the handful that survive the kill.

**The kill itself should be a grounding check, not a holistic vibe.** The reliable move against "pattern cosplay" is to require each proposed pattern to cite the census rows it eliminates and reject any that cannot — this converts an un-oracled judgment ("is this design good?") into a verifiable one ("does this citation map to real eliminated complexity?"). This is the same grounding-in-source discipline the prior pool entry found decisive for oracles: CANDOR derived oracles from natural-language docstrings rather than trusting the artifact, and panel disagreement was treated as signal (>70% disagreement rate, 0.980 mutation score) [12].

**Authority bias is the specific failure mode this gate must resist.** LLM judges credit citations of famous sources — including *fabricated* ones — inflating scores for answers that name-drop authority. This is exactly "pattern cosplay": a design that says "Strategy pattern, per GoF" without earning it. Judges' robustness to authority manipulation runs only 0.63–0.88 [6], so presence-of-a-famous-name must never be scored; only citation-that-grounds-in-census may be.

**Panels help, with sharp diminishing returns and an aggregation trap.** A 3-model heterogeneous panel beats a single GPT-4 judge on human correlation and cost, and structurally cancels self-preference [3]; but correlated errors collapse a 9-judge panel to ~2 effective votes [4], and naive mean aggregation has unbounded bias if even one judge fails in a typical biased way [5]. Small, diverse, robustly-aggregated beats large.

## Finding

- Pairwise/comparative judging approximates human preference with higher fidelity than pointwise absolute scoring on subjective dimensions, because relative judgments are easier and pointwise scalars fluctuate more [8]
- But pairwise is substantially more vulnerable to adversarial gaming: injected distractor features flip pairwise preferences in ~35% of cases vs only ~9% for absolute scores; absolute scoring "better reflects response quality and is less influenced by distractor features," so the feedback protocol itself induces systematic bias [2]
- The pairwise-vs-pointwise study concludes protocol choice should follow the evaluation objective — pairwise for human-alignment/subjective ranking, absolute where adversarial robustness and gaming-resistance dominate [2]
- Zheng et al. (MT-Bench / Chatbot Arena, the founding LLM-as-judge paper) documented four core judge failures: position bias, verbosity bias, self-enhancement bias, and limited reasoning; even GPT-4 reached the same verdict under both answer orderings only ~66% of the time, and judges preferred a losslessly-lengthened rephrase >90% of the time (verbosity) [1]
- The CALM framework ("Justice or Prejudice?") quantifies 12 biases; authority bias (crediting citations, including fake ones, to books/quotes/URLs) showed robustness of only 0.628–0.884, and position bias was weakest overall at 0.566–0.832 — both more exploitable than verbosity (0.884–0.977) [6]
- CALM's bandwagon effect (following a stated majority) dropped robustness to 0.599 at 90% claimed popularity, and self-enhancement error rates ran 1.16–16.1% across judges [6]
- Self-preference is real and mechanistic: LLM evaluators recognize their own generations, and there is a linear correlation between a model's self-recognition ability and the strength of its self-preference bias — i.e., judging your own output inflates the score (NeurIPS 2024) [7]
- A complementary account traces self-preference to perplexity/familiarity: judges assign higher scores to lower-perplexity text, and a model's own outputs are necessarily lower-perplexity to it [11]
- A 3-model panel of disparate families (Command R 35B + Claude 3 Haiku + GPT-3.5) beat a single GPT-4 judge — Cohen's κ 0.763 vs 0.627 on Natural Questions, Pearson 0.917 vs 0.817 on Chatbot Arena Hard — while being 7–8× cheaper, and structurally cancelled self-preference (each model's largest positive scoring delta occurs when it judges itself) [3]
- Panel gains hit hard diminishing returns from correlated errors: nine LLM judges provided only ~two effective independent votes, because judges systematically fail on the same cases, breaking the wisdom-of-crowds independence assumption [4]
- Naive panel aggregation is fragile: under a Huber contamination model, the mean's conditional bias cannot be bounded regardless of jury size once a single judge fails in a biased LLM-typical way (mode collapse, sycophancy, heavy-tailed hallucination); parser failures alone reached 33% on a small judge [5]
- Robust aggregation fixes this: replacing the mean with the geometric median (Weiszfeld iteration) is tuning-free, tolerates up to 50% corrupted judges, and gave ~19% improvement on cross-dimensional attacks at matched compute; a 38B 3-judge robust committee beat a 675B single judge by 1.31× under 30% corruption [5]
- Reject-by-default judges are naturally conservative: LLM judges in the jailbreak/harm-scoring literature show high precision (0.81–0.94) but low recall (0.06–0.65) — when they flag a failure they are usually right, but they miss large, unpredictable fractions of real failures; Best-of-N-style attacks disproportionately exploit judge false positives [8]
- For a killer/reward judge the error that matters flips by use: a false *positive* (passing an invalid proposal) assigns unearned reward, so precision is prized in reward modeling; a false *negative* (a missed bad proposal) is the reject-by-default gate's real cost, and low judge recall means a pure-judgment kill will let pattern-cosplay slip through unless the kill criterion is made verifiable [8]
- Rubric-anchored (analytic, named-criteria) judging decomposes quality into independently scored dimensions, reduces ambiguity, and yields interpretable dimension-level diagnosis that a single holistic scalar cannot — G-Eval exposed that "a single scalar cannot explain its own rationale or support fine-grained diagnosis," and its chain-of-thought-over-criteria step improves scoring consistency [10]
- With well-designed rubrics and behavioral anchors (few-shot exemplar verdicts, option labels as anchors), strong LLM judges reach 80–90% agreement with human evaluators — comparable to human inter-annotator agreement — on many quality dimensions [13]
- Rubric quality is the bottleneck and rubric-level errors propagate through aggregation: named-criteria correlation with humans is strong on subjective/conversational dimensions (ρ ≈ 0.8–0.9) but collapses on factual/domain-specific criteria (ρ ≈ 0.5 for summarization, below 0.3 for biomedical), so criteria tied to hard technical facts are the least reliable rubric rows [9]
- Prior pool knowledge (spec-driven-test-generation): CANDOR's multi-agent panel derived oracles from natural-language descriptions rather than the artifact, achieved 0.980 mutation score, and treated panel disagreement as signal with a >70% disagreement rate; contamination (a judge implicitly mirroring the artifact it should critique) was the primary validity threat [12]

## Fix

- Make stage 3 a two-tier gate: an ABSOLUTE, rubric-anchored reject-by-default kill first (robust to gaming), then pairwise/comparative scoring ONLY among survivors — this buys pairwise's human-alignment for the final ranking without exposing the kill decision to pairwise's 35%-flip gameability [2][8]
- Encode the kill as a grounding check, not a holistic score: a proposed pattern survives only if it cites specific complexity-census rows it eliminates, and is rejected-with-reason otherwise — this converts an un-oracled "is it good design?" into a verifiable "does the citation map to real eliminated complexity?", mirroring CANDOR/SPECA grounding-in-source [12]
- Score authority/name-dropping at zero: never let the presence of a famous pattern name (GoF, "hexagonal", "CQRS") add points; require the census-row grounding, because judges credit even fabricated authority citations (robustness 0.63–0.88) — this is precisely the pattern-cosplay attack surface [6]
- Never let the judge be (or know) the generator: use a judge model from a different family than stage-2 candidate generators, and strip provenance, because self-recognition drives self-preference and a model inflates its own designs [3][7]
- Anchor the rubric on named, checkable criteria the brief already defines — paths eliminated, migration safety, precedent fit — with per-criterion behavioral anchors and few-shot exemplar verdicts drawn from the PLN-1 reference brief; behavioral anchors and few-shot exemplars are what lift rubric agreement toward the 80–90% human-parity band [10][13]
- Calibrate the kill threshold toward recall on unjustified patterns, accepting some false-kills: rejections-with-reasons are the stated output value, and LLM judges' default posture is high-precision/low-recall, so the gate must be pushed to catch cosplay rather than left at its conservative default [8]
- If using a panel, keep it small (3) and disparate-family, and aggregate with geometric-median (or a trimmed/median rule), not the arithmetic mean — the mean has unbounded bias under one contaminated judge and 9 correlated judges add almost nothing [3][4][5]
- Run each pairwise survivor comparison in both orderings and average/require-consistency to neutralize position bias (swap-augmented evaluation), since even GPT-4 agrees with itself across orderings only ~66% of the time [1]

## Alternatives

- Single strong judge with a well-designed rubric: simplest and cheapest; strong judges hit 80–90% human agreement with good rubrics, so a panel may be unnecessary if the rubric and grounding gate are tight — but you forgo the self-preference cancellation a heterogeneous panel gives for free [13][3]
- Pure pairwise tournament among all candidates (no absolute gate): maximizes human-preference alignment and is the Chatbot-Arena-style default, but it cannot express "kill all of them" (it always yields a winner) and is the most gameable protocol (35% distractor flips) — wrong shape for a reject-by-default gate [2][8]
- Debate-style adjudication (candidates argue, judge decides): "debate helps weak judges reward stronger models," an option when the judge is weaker than the generators; heavier to run and not needed if the judge is a frontier model and the kill is grounding-checked [8]
- Large panel + majority vote: intuitive but defeated by correlated errors (9 judges → ~2 effective votes) and by mean-aggregation's unbounded contamination bias — dominated by small-diverse-panel + robust aggregation [4][5]
- Holistic single-scalar scoring: cheapest to prompt but cannot justify itself or support the rejections-with-reasons the brief requires; the rubric literature specifically flags the un-explainable scalar as the failure G-Eval/analytic rubrics were built to fix [10]

## Caveat

- Nearly all primary evidence is on NLG, chat-response, code-correctness, or harm/jailbreak judging — NOT on judging software *design* quality specifically. The direction of every finding is expected to transfer, but the exact magnitudes (bias robustness rates, agreement percentages) are not measured on design briefs; treat this as strong analogy, not direct measurement.
- Bias magnitudes are model- and version-dependent: CALM/Zheng numbers are from the GPT-4/Claude-3-era judges; a newer frontier judge may be more or differently biased. Recalibrate on the PLN-1 reference brief rather than trusting the literature's absolute numbers.
- Rubric anchoring is *least* reliable exactly where design judgment gets technical: criteria tied to hard facts (does this migration actually preserve behavior?) showed ρ<0.3 in the factual/domain regime, so the census-grounding check — not the LLM's opinion of "migration safety" — must carry the load for fact-heavy criteria [9].
- Reject-by-default calibration is a live tension: pushing recall up (catch all cosplay) raises false-kills of legitimately-justified-but-terse proposals; the census-row grounding requirement is what keeps false-kills defensible (a killed proposal genuinely lacked a mapped citation), but a genuinely good design with a *poorly-worded* citation can still be wrongly killed.
- "Two effective votes" and the contamination bounds are recent (2026) results; the specific effective-vote count depends on how correlated your chosen judges are — disparate families reduce, but do not eliminate, correlation [4][5].

## Fallback

- If the absolute grounding gate over-kills (rejects good designs on citation wording): loosen from "must cite the exact census row" to "must cite eliminated complexity the skeptic can re-derive from the census," keeping the grounding discipline while tolerating imperfect phrasing — the point is verifiability, not literal string match [12]
- If a single judge proves unreliable on the calibration brief: add a 3-judge disparate-family panel with geometric-median aggregation before reaching for anything larger; stop adding judges once marginal agreement flattens (correlated-error ceiling) [3][4][5]
- If pairwise ranking of survivors proves gameable by a verbose or authority-laden candidate: switch the survivor ranking to absolute rubric scores too (accept slightly worse human-alignment for the 9%-vs-35% robustness gain), since survivors already passed the kill and the remaining task is ordering, not gatekeeping [2]
- If authority/name-dropping still leaks through: add an explicit adversarial prompt instruction to distrust pattern names and score only census-grounded justifications, and spot-check by planting a fabricated-authority candidate in calibration (CALM's methodology) to measure the judge's actual authority robustness on your briefs [6]
- If self-preference is suspected (judge favoring one generator's house style): rotate the judge model family and re-run; a stable ranking across judge families indicates the ranking is not a self-recognition artifact [3][7]

## Recommendation

**Immediate next action for mine-design stage 3:** Build it as a two-tier adversarial gate, not a scorer. Tier 1 is an absolute, reject-by-default *grounding* kill: for each proposed pattern, the judge must locate the complexity-census row(s) it eliminates; a pattern that cannot ground its citation is killed-with-reason. This is the single highest-leverage design choice — it sidesteps the un-oracled subjectivity problem entirely by making the kill *verifiable* (mirroring CANDOR/SPECA), and it directly defeats authority/name-dropping bias, which is the literature's most exploitable relevant bias and the exact shape of "pattern cosplay." Tier 2 comparatively (pairwise) ranks only the survivors against the PLN-1 reference brief's named criteria (paths eliminated, migration safety, precedent fit), run in both orderings to cancel position bias.

Use a frontier judge from a **different family than the stage-2 generators**, strip provenance, and calibrate the kill threshold toward recall (catching cosplay) using PLN-1 plus a planted fabricated-authority candidate to measure actual authority robustness. Add a 3-judge disparate-family panel with geometric-median aggregation only if the single judge proves unstable on calibration — do not reach for a large panel (correlated errors make it worthless, and mean aggregation is contamination-fragile).

**Next probe if reopened:** search for direct empirical work on LLM judging of *software design/architecture* quality specifically (the current gap — all evidence here is analogized from NLG/chat/code-correctness/harm judging), and for any "verifiable-criteria reject-by-default" reward-modeling result that measures false-kill vs false-pass on grounded-citation gates.
